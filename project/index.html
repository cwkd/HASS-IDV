Charlie Hebdo Online Discussion

In the wake of the Charlie Hebdo killings, there emerged many threads of discussion on Twitter and along with them, the spread of rumours related to the event. The visualisations below aim to allow users to explore the various tweet trees along with other meta-information pertaining to the event.

User Influence

In any event, a small minority tends to dominate the discussion, the influence of each user is shown below, indicating the number of reactions spawned by the user's tweets along with who they interacted with most and the number of interactions.

Users Activity Trends

Besides looking at the user influence, we are also interested in user activity in aggregate, thus we have a stacked histogram with user tweet, link and thread counts. In general, we observe that most users are not very active only having 1 or 2 tweets, in addition from the users are generally only active in 1 or 2 threads and have few links from their tweets to other tweets. However, there are still some users who are more active and garner more attention this is represented by the relatively greater share of links versus tweets and threads in the bins with higher counts.

Tweet Trees

Finally, we want to look at the structures of the various tweet trees to see if there are differences in the types of conversations happening. Mouseover individual nodes to see tweet details.
